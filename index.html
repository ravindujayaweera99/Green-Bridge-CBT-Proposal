Needs Assessment

The educational landscape in West Africa faces a myriad of challenges that significantly hinder the academic progress and future opportunities of secondary school students. The existing education systems in many countries within the region are characterized by outdated curricula, inadequate practical learning resources, and a lack of digital literacy training. These issues are compounded by insufficient guidance and counseling services, as well as a recent shift toward computer-based testing formats that many students are ill-prepared to navigate. This confluence of factors has contributed to alarmingly low pass rates in critical examinations, limiting students' access to higher education and their ability to compete in a rapidly evolving global economy. Green Bridge CBT seeks to address these pressing issues by providing a comprehensive digital learning platform that bridges these educational gaps, equipping students with the tools and knowledge they need to succeed.

Education in West Africa has historically been marked by a focus on rote memorization and theoretical instruction, often at the expense of practical skills and critical thinking. The curricula in many countries remain largely unchanged, failing to incorporate the advancements in technology and educational methodologies that have been embraced in other parts of the world. This has left students ill-prepared to meet the demands of modern society and the global job market. Additionally, schools in the region frequently lack the necessary equipment and resources to facilitate hands-on learning, further exacerbating the disconnect between theoretical knowledge and real-world application.

The transition to computer-based testing by major examination boards, such as the West African Examination Council (W AEC) and the Joint Admission and Matriculation Board (JAMB), has introduced new challenges for students. While this shift represents a necessary modernization of the examination process, it has left many students struggling due to their limited access to digital technology and lack of familiarity with digital exam formats. Inadequate infrastructure, such as a lack of computers and reliable internet access, poses significant barriers to students' ability to practice and prepare for these exams effectively.

Moreover, the role of guidance and counseling services in schools has been diminished, depriving students of essential support in navigating their academic and career paths. Many students are left without the necessary information to make informed decisions about their future, leading to poor subject choices and limited career prospects. The absence of adequate guidance further exacerbates the challenges faced by students in an already challenging educational environment.

The need for intervention in the West African education system is underscored by a wealth of evidence, including statistics and reports that highlight the severity of the issues at hand. According to UNESCO, over 89% of students in Sub-Saharan Africa lack access to computers, and 82% are without reliable internet connectivity. This digital divide places students at a significant disadvantage, particularly as examinations transition to computer-based formats. Without the necessary tools to practice and prepare, many students struggle to adapt to these new testing methods, resulting in poor performance and increased failure rates.

The statistics from recent examinations paint a stark picture of the challenges faced by students in the region. In Sierra Leone, the pass rate for the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (W ASSCE) was just 3.81% in 2020, indicating that the vast majority of students failed to achieve the requisite grades for higher education admission. Similarly, Liberia recorded a pass rate of only 0.05% in 2020, with just 25 out of over 41,000 students passing the required five subjects, including Mathematics and English. These figures are indicative of the widespread educational deficiencies that exist in the region and the urgent need for targeted intervention. In The Gambia, the pass rate has consistently remained below 6% over the past several years, further highlighting the systemic challenges faced by students. Even in Ghana, where the educational system is relatively more advanced, only around 65% of students meet the required criteria for university admission. In Nigeria, while there is a reasonable success rate for the W AEC exams, the performance in the JAMB Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) remains concerning, with over 65% of students consistently scoring less than 50%. The high failure rates in these exams have far-reaching implications for students' future prospects, as they limit access to higher education and, consequently, to better employment opportunities. The inability to secure admission to universities forces many students to repeat examinations, placing additional financial and emotional burdens on themselves and their families. This cycle of repeated failure further entrenches educational inequities and limits the potential for social and economic mobility.

Beyond the statistics, qualitative reports from educators and students underscore the challenges faced by those navigating the West African education system. Educators report that students often struggle to grasp complex concepts due to a lack of practical experience and exposure to real-world applications. The absence of modern teaching tools and resources further compounds these challenges, leaving students ill-prepared to compete with their peers in other parts of the world.

In response to these pressing challenges, Green Bridge CBT proposes a comprehensive solution that leverages technology to bridge the gaps in the current education system. Our platform is designed to provide students with access to high-quality digital resources, including interactive lesson notes, video tutorials, and bite-sized podcasts. By offering the largest database of past examination questions in Africa, Green Bridge CBT enables students to practice and prepare effectively, building their confidence and enhancing their performance in exams.

Furthermore, the integration of extended reality (XR) laboratories allows students to gain practical experience through virtual experiments, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and real-world application. Our platform also includes personalized guidance and counseling services, providing students with the support they need to make informed decisions about their academic and career paths.

Implementation Plan

The implementation of Green Bridge CBT will be carried out in three main phases, each focusing on specific goals and objectives to ensure the successful development and deployment of the platform. This structured approach will allow us to manage resources efficiently, track progress, and achieve our desired outcomes within the proposed timeline.

Phase 1: Development and Enhancement (Months 1-12)

This phase is crucial for building the foundational components of the Green Bridge CBT platform. The focus will be on developing key features and content that address the educational needs of students in West Africa.

Milestone: Create and produce 700 microlearning videos covering essential high school topics.
Timeline: Months 1-6
Description: Microlearning videos will provide concise, engaging content to enhance understanding and retention. Topics will include Mathematics, Science, and Humanities, focusing on critical areas that align with examination syllabi.

Milestone: Develop virtual labs for 200 practical topics across various subjects.
Timeline: Months 4-12
Description: The XR labs will simulate real-world experiments in subjects like Chemistry, Physics, and Biology. These labs will provide hands-on experience, allowing students to apply theoretical knowledge in a virtual environment.

Milestone: Hire and onboard 50 personal tutors and counselors.
Timeline: Months 3-8
Description: Tutors will offer personalized academic support, while counselors will provide guidance on career planning and academic choices, ensuring students receive comprehensive support.

Phase 2: Platform Expansion and Accessibility (Months 13-18)

The second phase focuses on expanding the platform's reach by developing mobile and desktop applications to ensure accessibility across various devices.

Milestone: Launch the Green Bridge CBT app on the Apple App Store.
Timeline: Months 13-15
Description: The iOS app will provide a seamless user experience for students using Apple devices, ensuring access to all platform features and resources.

Milestone: Release a desktop version of the platform for Windows and macOS.
Timeline: Months 14-17
Description: The desktop application will offer a native-friendly experience for students accessing the platform from computers, enhancing usability and accessibility.

Milestone: Update the existing Android app to include all web platform features.
Timeline: Months 16-18
Description: The enhancement will ensure that Android users have access to the full range of resources, including lesson notes and video tutorials.

Phase 3: Marketing and Outreach (Months 19-24)

The final phase focuses on marketing and outreach efforts to increase platform visibility, attract new users, and establish strategic partnerships.

Milestone: Implement a multi-channel marketing strategy across social media, digital advertising, and educational networks.
Timeline: Months 19-24
Description: The campaign will target students, educators, and parents, highlighting the benefits of Green Bridge CBT and encouraging platform adoption.

Milestone: Partner with 50 schools across West Africa.
Timeline: Months 20-24
Description: Strategic partnerships will ensure the platform is integrated into school curricula, providing students with a comprehensive learning experience.

Evaluation Plan

The success of Green Bridge CBT hinges on a robust evaluation plan that measures progress, ensures accountability, and informs continuous improvement. Our evaluation framework is designed to assess the impact of the project on educational outcomes, identify areas for enhancement, and ensure transparency in reporting to stakeholders and funders. To effectively evaluate the impact of Green Bridge CBT, we have identified a set of key metrics that align with our project objectives. These metrics will serve as indicators of success and guide our efforts in achieving the desired outcomes. First, student engagement and usage will be tracked by monitoring the number of active users, frequency of platform access, and duration of engagement. This data will provide insights into student interest and satisfaction with our resources. Additionally, we will monitor changes in students’ examination scores and pass rates to measure the effectiveness of our learning tools in enhancing academic performance. Another critical metric is the improvement in digital literacy and skills development. We aim to assess students' progress in navigating computer-based tests and using digital resources, which will demonstrate the platform’s impact on preparing students for modern exam formats. We will also gather user feedback and satisfaction through surveys and feedback forms, providing qualitative data on the user experience and areas for further development. Lastly, the number and quality of partnerships established with educational institutions and government agencies will be evaluated to reflect our success in expanding the platform’s reach and influence. To ensure comprehensive and accurate evaluation, we will employ a variety of monitoring and evaluation methods, including both quantitative and qualitative approaches. Data analytics tools will be utilized to collect and analyze data on user engagement, exam performance, and digital literacy improvements. This quantitative data will offer valuable insights into the platform’s impact and help identify trends over time. We will also conduct regular surveys and feedback sessions with students, educators, and parents to gather qualitative data on user satisfaction and areas for improvement, which will inform iterative enhancements to the platform. In addition, focus groups and interviews with key stakeholders will be organized to gain deeper insights into their experiences and perceptions of the platform. These discussions will provide valuable context and inform strategic decision-making. We will prepare regular progress reports that summarize key findings from our evaluation activities, highlight successes and challenges, and outline plans for addressing identified issues.

Accountability is a cornerstone of our evaluation plan, ensuring transparency and trust with our stakeholders and funders. We are committed to maintaining open communication and providing timely updates on project progress and outcomes. Quarterly reports will be submitted to funders and stakeholders, detailing project activities, achievements, and any challenges encountered. These reports will include data-driven insights and highlight key milestones reached. An annual impact report will provide a comprehensive overview of the project’s progress and outcomes, including an analysis of metrics for success, case studies of student experiences, and a summary of partnerships and collaborations. Furthermore, we will maintain transparent financial records and provide regular updates on budget utilization and expenditures, ensuring funders have confidence in the responsible management of resources.
